SciBlitz AI Challenge is the flagship AI competition of SciBlitz, the national
technology festival organized by the IEEE Student Branch of Chittagong University of
Engineering & Technology (CUET). The competition invites student teams from across
Bangladesh to design and build AI-powered products that address real-world
problems.

3. Eligibility
The competition is open to all currently enrolled students at any university, college,        
or polytechnic institute in Bangladesh.
Teams may include members from different institutions.
Students from any discipline are welcome, you do not need to be from a computer

serve as mentors only.

5. Project Requirements
5.1 Core requirement
Artificial intelligence or machine learning must be a central and meaningful component of your
project; not a peripheral feature. A project that merely calls an API without any meaningful
integration, customization, or application to a problem will not be considered compliant.
5.2 Originality & development window
The project must be original work as per this rulebook.
Your GitHub repository must show commits made during the competition window (May 14–July
1, 2026). Repositories with no commits in this period will be flagged.
5.3 Permitted tools & technologies
Any programming language, framework, or cloud platform is allowed.
Use of pre-trained models, foundation models (e.g. GPT-4, Gemini, LLaMA), and fine-tuned
models is fully permitted and encouraged.
Public APIs (OpenAI, Hugging Face, Google AI, etc.) are allowed. Usage costs are the team’s
responsibility.
Open-source libraries and datasets are allowed. All third-party resources must be properly
attributed.
No-code/low-code AI tools (e.g. Bubble, FlutterFlow with AI plugins) are permitted for Track D
only, provided the report clearly explains the AI logic and the team can answer technical
questions during judging.
5.4 Live demo requirement
Your project must be deployed and accessible via a public URL. Judges will evaluate your product
directly in the browser. No local setup, installation, or login should be required to access the core
demo.
Accepted hosting platforms include (not limited to):
Hugging Face Spaces — recommended for ML/Gradio/Streamlit apps
Streamlit Community Cloud
Vercel or Netlify — for frontend-heavy apps
Render — for full-stack apps with a backend

8.1 Evaluation notes
Technical Implementation does not require training a model from scratch. Effective,
appropriate, and well-integrated use of existing tools and APIs will score well.
Demo Quality is evaluated on your live hosted URL and demo video. A broken demo
will result in a significantly lower score for this criterion.
Innovation is assessed relative to the problem context — a novel approach to a local
problem can score higher than a technically complex but routine solution.
Presentation score applies only to teams in the Final Day. Remote teams’
communication scores are based on the report and video.
8.2 Shortlisting
After the submission deadline, the top 20 teams by aggregate score will be shortlisted for
the Final Day. Shortlisted teams will be notified by July 3, 2026. The shortlist decision is
final.

9. Final Day — July 10, 2026
9.1 Format
Each shortlisted team will receive 5 minutes to present followed by 3 minutes of Q&A
from the judges.
Teams may demonstrate their live product during the presentation slot.
9.2 Attendance
At least one team member must be physically present at the venue on July 10.
Remaining members may participate remotely via video call, subject to venue
connectivity.
Teams must be available and ready for the full final day schedule. Presentation order
will be communicated to shortlisted teams on July 9.
Failure to present at the assigned time without prior notice will result in
disqualification from the final.
9.3 Technical preparation
Check your live demo URL on the evening of July 9.
Some platforms (e.g. Hugging Face Spaces on free tier) sleep after inactivity; visit your app
to wake it up.
Bring a backup: have your demo video ready in case of live demo failure.
Bring your slides on a USB drive as backup.
10. Rules & Conduct
10.1 Prohibited actions
Plagiarism of code, ideas, text, or visual content without proper attribution.
Using another team’s work, or having a non-team-member contribute to the project.
Misrepresenting the capabilities of your product in the demo video or presentation.
Registering as part of more than one team.
Harassment, disrespect, or disruptive conduct toward other participants, judges, or
organizers, whether online or in person.
Attempting to contact or influence judges regarding scoring.

10.3 Intellectual property
Teams retain full ownership of their projects and all intellectual property created
during the competition.
By registering, teams grant IEEE Student Branch CUET a non-exclusive, royalty-free
license to feature their project name, description, and demo screenshots in SciBlitz
promotional and documentation materials.
IEEE SB CUET will not commercially use or redistribute any team’s codebase without
explicit written permission.
10.4 Disqualification
The organizing committee reserves the right to disqualify any team found to be in violation of
these rules, at any stage of the competition. Disqualification decisions are final. In the event of
disqualification after fee payment, the registration fee is non-refundable.
11. Prizes & Recognition
All third-party code, models, datasets, and libraries used must be listed in the
README and Model & Data Card.
Use of open-source code is permitted provided the applicable license is respected
and attribution is given.
Violation of a third-party license is grounds for disqualification.
Prize details will be announced via official SciBlitz channels, stay tuned. All participating
teams that submit a complete project package will receive:
       A digital certificate of participation from IEEE Student Branch, CUET
       Digital recognition for the finalists in all SciBlitz Platform.
Shortlisted and winning teams will receive additional certificates and recognition as
announced.
